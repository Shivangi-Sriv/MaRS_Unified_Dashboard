STUDENT HANDBOOK — ACADEMIC YEAR 2026

ATTENDANCE POLICY
A minimum of 75% attendance is mandatory in every registered course. Students whose attendance falls below 75% will be detained from the end-semester examination for that course. Attendance between 65% and 75% may be condoned only on medical grounds with a certificate submitted to the academic office within seven days of rejoining. Attendance is calculated separately for lectures, tutorials, and laboratory sessions.

EXAMINATION RULES
The assessment for each course consists of two mid-semester examinations weighted 20% each, continuous assessment weighted 20%, and an end-semester examination weighted 40%. A student must secure at least 40% aggregate to pass a course. Use of unfair means during an examination results in a zero in that course and referral to the disciplinary committee. Re-examination is available only for students who miss an exam with prior approved leave.

GRADING SYSTEM
Grades are awarded on a 10-point scale: A+ (10), A (9), B (8), C (7), D (6), E (5), and F (fail). The Semester Grade Point Average is computed as the credit-weighted average of grade points. A cumulative average below 5.0 places a student on academic probation.

FEE PAYMENT
Semester fees must be paid before the start of each semester. A late fee of 500 rupees per week applies after the deadline, up to a maximum of four weeks, after which registration is blocked. Hostel and mess fees are billed separately each semester. Refunds for withdrawal are processed on a pro-rata basis as per the institute refund schedule.

HOSTEL RULES
Hostel residents must return to the hostel by 10:30 PM on weekdays and 11:30 PM on weekends. Overnight leave requires prior approval through the warden portal. Cooking in rooms, use of electrical heaters, and possession of prohibited items are not permitted. Ragging in any form is a criminal offence and leads to immediate expulsion.

LIBRARY RULES
Undergraduate students may borrow up to four books for a period of fourteen days. Reference books and journals cannot be borrowed and must be used within the library. A fine of 5 rupees per day applies for overdue books. Lost books must be replaced or paid for at twice the current market price.

LEAVE AND MEDICAL POLICY
Medical leave requires a certificate from a registered practitioner submitted within seven days. Duty leave for representing the institute in approved events is granted with prior sanction and does not count against attendance. Casual leave is not applicable to students.

GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
Students may raise academic or administrative grievances through the online grievance portal. Complaints are acknowledged within three working days and resolved within fifteen working days. Anti-ragging and anti-harassment complaints are handled by dedicated committees with strict confidentiality.
